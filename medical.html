MEDICAL COURSES



MBBS-MD

There is a shortage of doctors in the health sector today. Becoming a good doctor is the dream of many. The reason for this is that the doctor has a recognized status in the society. It should have not less than 50% marks each in Physics, Chemistry, Biology and English subject in +2 Science subject. Also in NEET, which is an all India exam, general category should score 50% and OBC 40%.


BDS-MDS

To pursue a Master's in Dental Surgery (MDS) after completing a Bachelor's in Dental Surgery (BDS), candidates must secure at least 50% in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English during their higher secondary education. They need to pass the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Dental (NEET MDS) with a minimum of 50% for general category and meet specific percentages for other categories. After completing BDS, a successful internship, and the NEET MDS exam, candidates can specialize in areas such as Orthodontics or Prosthodontics during their MDS program.


VETERINARY

To pursue a Master's in Veterinary Surgery (MVSc) after completing a Bachelor's in Veterinary Science and Animal Husbandry (BVSc & AH), candidates need at least 50% in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English in their higher secondary education. They must pass the relevant entrance exams, such as All India Pre-Veterinary Test (AIPVT), with specified percentages for different categories. After completing BVSc & AH, a successful internship, and the entrance exam, candidates can specialize in areas like Surgery or Medicine during their MVSc program.


BAMS-MD AYURVEDA

To pursue a Doctor of Medicine in Ayurveda (MD Ayurveda) after completing a Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS), candidates need a minimum of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English in their higher secondary education. They must also clear the relevant entrance exams, such as All India Ayush Post Graduate Entrance Test (AIAPGET), with specific percentages for different categories. Following completion of BAMS, a successful internship, and the entrance exam, candidates can specialize in areas like Kayachikitsa or Shalya Tantra during their MD Ayurveda program.


BHMS

To pursue a Doctor of Medicine in Homeopathy (MD Homeopathy) after completing a Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine and Surgery (BHMS), candidates must secure a minimum of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English during their higher secondary education. They need to clear relevant entrance exams, such as the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Homeopathy (NEET Homeopathy), with specific percentage requirements for various categories. After completing BHMS, a successful internship, and the entrance exam, candidates can specialize in areas like Materia Medica or Organon of Medicine during their MD Homeopathy program.


BYNS

To advance in the field of Naturopathy and Yogic Sciences, individuals with a BNYS degree typically pursue postgraduate studies. Admission to programs like a Master's in Naturopathy and Yogic Sciences (MNY) often requires a minimum of 50% in BNYS, along with meeting specific criteria in entrance exams. After successfully completing BNYS, including practical training, internships, and qualifying exams, students can specialize in areas like Naturopathic Medicine or Yoga Therapy during their postgraduate studies.


BUMS

Aspiring Unani medicine practitioners pursue a Bachelor of Unani Medicine and Surgery (BUMS) degree. Eligibility often requires a minimum of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English during higher secondary education. Successful completion of the BUMS program, including internships and practical training, allows graduates to practice Unani medicine. For specialization or advanced studies, individuals may explore postgraduate options such as MD (Doctor of Medicine) in Unani Medicine.


BSMS

Students aspiring to practice Siddha medicine pursue a Bachelor of Siddha Medicine and Surgery (BSMS) degree. Admission typically requires a minimum of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English during higher secondary education. After completing the BSMS program, including practical training and internships, graduates are equipped to practice Siddha medicine. For those seeking specialization or advanced studies, options such as MD (Doctor of Medicine) in Siddha Medicine may be available.


PHARM D

Aspiring pharmacists pursue a Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm D) degree. Eligibility often requires completion of higher secondary education with a focus on science subjects. The Pharm D program includes comprehensive education in pharmaceutical sciences, clinical pharmacy, and patient care. Graduates are equipped to work in various healthcare settings, contributing to medication management and patient well-being. Pharm D is a professional doctorate, and upon completion, individuals can practice as clinical pharmacists or pursue further specialization through postgraduate studies.


AGRICULTURE

An agriculture degree equips individuals with the knowledge and skills to address global challenges in food production, sustainability, and environmental conservation. Graduates delve into agronomy, crop science, and sustainable farming practices. With a growing demand for food security and sustainable agriculture, the scope of agriculture degrees is vast. Careers span from agribusiness management to research and development, offering opportunities in improving crop yields, mitigating climate impact, and shaping the future of global food systems.


FORESTRY

A forestry degree provides expertise in managing and conserving forests, addressing crucial environmental issues. Graduates delve into subjects like forest ecology, conservation, and resource management. The scope of forestry degrees is diverse, offering careers in sustainable forestry, wildlife management, and environmental consulting. As the world focuses on ecological balance and climate change mitigation, forestry professionals play a crucial role in sustainable resource utilization and biodiversity conservation, making forestry degrees highly relevant and impactful.


FISHERIES

A fisheries degree focuses on the sustainable management of aquatic resources and ecosystems. Graduates gain expertise in aquaculture, marine biology, and fisheries management. The scope of fisheries degrees extends to diverse career paths, including fisheries research, aquaculture development, and marine conservation. With the increasing demand for seafood and the need to balance ecological concerns, fisheries professionals contribute to the sustainable use of aquatic resources, ensuring both environmental conservation and economic viability.


DIARY SCIENCE

A degree in dairy science equips individuals with knowledge in dairy production, animal husbandry, and dairy technology. Graduates delve into areas such as milk processing, quality control, and animal nutrition. The scope of dairy science degrees extends to careers in dairy farming, milk processing industries, and research. As the demand for high-quality dairy products grows, professionals in dairy science play a vital role in ensuring efficient and sustainable production practices, contributing to both agricultural and economic development.
